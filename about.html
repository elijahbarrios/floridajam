The Florida Jam is a new outreach project for youth, sponsored by the Florida State Fiddlers Association. This program is focused on teaching youth (children under the age of 18) traditional old time, blue grass and Celtic music. We offer group workshops and jams at community centers and area venues and we welcome everyone to participate! Currently, we have 2 pilot projects going - one in South Florida and one in North Florida, and we hope to expand across the state in the future. We are thankful to have support for our Tallahassee programs from the city of Tallahassee, Leon County, and COCA.

Our team

Aisha Ivey

Aisha Ivey earned her doctorate in music education from Florida State University. A champion fiddler, she has won the southeast United States Scottish Fiddle Championship for 10 years in a row (2006-2015). She has also won awards for her Irish, Old-Time and Scandinavian fiddle playing. Aisha is former president of The Florida State Fiddlers Association (2011-2015) and she teaches several groups including AZIZA! (Middle Eastern Music and Dance) and the Old Time Ensemble at Florida State University. She has recorded several CD's with different musicians and she has written some instructional fiddle books as well. Aisha is an active teacher, performer, researcher and promoter of traditional fiddle styles.

Andy Martin

Andy Martin discovered a love for string instruments around age 14. At first, he was learning a few chords on guitar, but he was soon to discover his true love: the BANJO! Andy taught himself to play the banjo, formed a bluegrass band, and was performing in Southern Georgia while still in high school. At the same time, he was involved in the band program in Tifton, GA where he became an accomplished trombonist. He went on to earn his bachelor’s degree in music education from Florida State University in 1989 and taught music in a public school band program for 5 years. In the late 90s, Andy helped form the critically acclaimed Dappled Grays, an Atlanta-based, new acoustic/bluegrass band that performed extensively throughout the Southeast. He also led 16 Tons, a traditional bluegrass band in Athens, GA. In the early 2000s, Andy came across an old fiddle that needed some work, so he decided to fix it up and learn to play fiddle. This led to a new interest in building/repairing string instruments. He loves “saving the life” of old instruments and getting them into the hands of players that can enjoy them! One of the best examples of his skills as a luthier are the banjos he builds and sells through Martin String Music. Andy was also inspired to build his own Thunderbucket: a variation on a wash-tub bass. In a jam session, you may see him playing banjo, fiddle, guitar, or bass! In addition to playing, Andy loves to sing. He has been writing and collecting songs from many genres for over 40 years. Bluegrass, folk, blues, and old-time songs have many themes, stories and characters, and Andy loves sharing these “poems put to music” with others. Andy teaches banjo, fiddle, and guitar privately and at music camps, and he is very grateful to be part of JAM Tallahassee! He is the current Vice President of the Florida Fiddlers Association and is the author of Banjo Kung Fu: The Art of Listening.
